# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Practicing dentists and clinic owners, primarily in India (billing in ₹), running solo
practices or small multi-chair clinics. Their context: they finish a clinical
consultation in minutes, then lose the rest of the day to documentation, prescriptions,
appointment scheduling, billing, and follow-ups. They reach this website tired of being
a data-entry operator after every patient. Secondary viewers: receptionists and clinic
managers who live in the queue and the billing.

State of mind on arrival: fatigued, slightly skeptical of "AI" software, hoping for
relief but braced for another dashboard-heavy tool that adds work instead of removing it.

The job to be done: convince a dentist, in one scroll, that Odovox gives their time back,
then get them to book a walkthrough.

## Product Purpose

Odovox is a voice-first dental operating system. The dentist speaks naturally during or
right after a consultation, and Odovox writes the records: diagnosis, prescription,
procedure, follow-up, and billing, before the patient leaves the chair. The website's
purpose is not to demo features. It is to dramatize the transformation of a dentist's day,
from typing-and-clicking to speaking, and to make booking a walkthrough feel inevitable.

Success looks like a dentist thinking "this gives me my time back" and clicking Book
Walkthrough.

## Brand Personality

Three words: **warm, tactile, operational.** Calm and human, never futuristic or flashy.
It should feel like a modern clinic that runs differently, not like a software company.
Confident and quiet. Premium the way good paper and good light are premium, not the way
neon gradients are.

Voice: plain, declarative, a little editorial. Short lines. It states the dentist's reality
back to them with empathy, then shows the relief. No hype, no "revolutionary AI" language,
no exclamation marks.

Emotional arc the site must produce, in order: recognition, frustration, relief, curiosity,
trust, desire.

## Anti-references

- Generic healthcare SaaS: white background, blue buttons, dashboard screenshots, identical
  feature cards. This is the primary thing to avoid.
- AI-startup aesthetics: neon gradients, dark-mode-by-default, glassmorphism, "the future of
  AI" copy, robot/sparkle iconography.
- Crypto / web3 palettes, futuristic chrome, sci-fi.
- Corporate stock illustration (generic smiling people, abstract blobs).
- Long paragraphs and brochure copy. The site tells stories visually with minimal text.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the day, not the software.** Every section answers "how does your day change?"
   not "look at our features." Workflow over feature-list.
2. **The enemy is repetition, not paper.** Reinforce one thesis everywhere: the burden is
   the typing, clicking, tab-switching, and re-entry after treatment. Odovox removes it.
3. **Speech is the interface; the software is invisible.** Voice is the hero interaction.
   The UI should recede so the patient and the work remain.
4. **Calm, engineered motion.** Motion is deliberate and physical: fields collapse into a
   microphone, timelines draw themselves, the queue flows. No bounce, no float, no startup
   confetti. If it feels like a startup animation, it is wrong.
5. **Honest by omission.** No fake reviews, fake dentist counts, or invented statistics.
   Credibility comes from showing the real workflow and the real app, not numbers.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. The warm-paper palette must keep text contrast at AA on every section
surface (sage, peach, mustard, dusty blue, warm grey, and the dark consult mode). Honor
`prefers-reduced-motion`: the scroll-scrubbed sequences must degrade to clear static states
that still tell the story. Full keyboard operability for nav and the booking CTA; visible
focus styles. Captions/working controls for embedded product video.
